Current Members

Professor Remco Chang

[V]ALT Advisor
E-mail: remco@cs.tufts.edu

Remco Chang is an Assistant Professor in the Computer Science Department at Tufts University. He received his B.S from Johns Hopkins University in 1997 in Computer Science and Economics, MSc from Brown University in 2000, and Ph.D. in computer science from UNC Charlotte in 2009. Prior to his Ph.D., he worked for Boeing developing real-time flight tracking and visualization software, followed by a position at UNC Charlotte as a research scientist. His current research includes visual analytics, information visualization, urban modeling, and computer graphics.

Lane Harrison

Research Interests: Emotion and Perception in Visualization; Security Visualization
E-mail: lane@cs.tufts.edu

Lane Harrison is a Postdoc in the Visual Analytics Lab @ Tufts. He received his Ph.D. from the University of North Carolina at Charlotte in 2013, where he investigated the role of emotion in visualization. Lane is also an active member in the field of security visualization, having completed two security visualization-focused internships at national labs (Pacific Northwest National Lab (2010), Oak Ridge National Lab (2012)), and serving on the program committee for the Visualization for Cyber Security Symposium (VizSec).

Eli T Brown

Research Interests: Visual Analytics, Machine Learning, Provenance, User Modeling
E-mail: ebrown@cs.tufts.edu

Eli is a Ph.D. candidate working at the intersection of machine learning and visualization. His work focuses on modeling: improving data models by learning from users' interactions with a visual system, learning about users from those same interactions, and using the in-progress models to help guide users, saving time and frustration for domain experts. Eli earned his B.A. at Cornell University in Math and Computer Science.

Alvitta Ottley

E-mail: alvittao@cs.tufts.edu

Alvitta Ottley is a graduate student pursuing a Ph.D in computer Science at Tufts University. Her research interests include Information Visualization and Human-Computer Interaction. She received her B.S. degree in Computer Science from the State University of New York at Plattsburgh.
