Current Members

Professor Remco Chang

[V]ALT Advisor
E-mail: remco@cs.tufts.edu

Remco Chang is an Assistant Professor in the Computer Science Department at Tufts University. He received his B.S from Johns Hopkins University in 1997 in Computer Science and Economics, MSc from Brown University in 2000, and Ph.D. in computer science from UNC Charlotte in 2009. Prior to his Ph.D., he worked for Boeing developing real-time flight tracking and visualization software, followed by a position at UNC Charlotte as a research scientist. His current research includes visual analytics, information visualization, urban modeling, and computer graphics.

Eli Brown

E-mail: ebrown@cs.tufts.edu

Eli is now in his fourth semester at Tufts, working at VALT (the Visual Analytics Laboratory at Tufts). His work focuses on the intersection of visualization and machine learning. He's interested in questions like how we can use visualization to help users interact with machine learners and we can use machine learning to build more useful visualizations.

Alumni

Connor Gramazio

Research Interests: Massive Data, Human-Centered Design
E-mail: connor.gramazio@tufts.edu

Connor is a Senior majoring in Computer Science. For his thesis he is looking at better ways to store geospatial-temporal massive data to expand the types of interactions and increase the feasibility of exploring massive data. In the past he has worked on software visualization, protein visualization, and an information visualization tool to help graphic designers. His interest in massive data focuses on designing new back-end design strategies to help power interaction and usability research. On a broader scale Connor hopes to find ways to promote further collaboration between interaction and visual designers with developers.
